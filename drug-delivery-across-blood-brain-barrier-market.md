<p><p>The drug delivery across the blood-brain barrier market encompasses various strategies. The Trojan Horse approach uses carrier molecules, often mimicking natural substances, to transport drugs into the brain. Bispecific antibody receptor-mediated transcytosis (RMT) employs antibodies that bind to receptors on the endothelial cells to facilitate drug entry. Increasing permeability techniques enhance the barrier's openness temporarily, allowing drugs through. Other methods include nanocarriers and focused ultrasound, providing innovative ways to improve therapeutic access to the central nervous system.</p></p>
