<p><p>Masonry veneer types include adhered, anchored, articulated, and others, each catering to different construction needs. Adhered veneer relies on adhesives for direct attachment to building surfaces, offering a seamless finish. Anchored veneer features mechanical ties for added structural support, suitable for taller structures. Articulated veneer incorporates design elements to enhance aesthetics while maintaining functionality. Other variants may include innovative materials or hybrid systems, providing flexibility in design and application. Together, these categories address aesthetic, structural, and performance requirements in modern construction.</p></p>
