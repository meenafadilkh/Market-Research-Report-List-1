<p><p>Corrosion-resistant coatings and paints are categorized into three main types based on their solvent content. Water-borne coatings use water as the primary solvent, making them environmentally friendly and easy to clean. Solvent-borne coatings, on the other hand, utilize organic solvents, offering superior adhesion and durability but often emit volatile organic compounds (VOCs). Solids market coatings contain no solvents or minimal solvent content, providing high-build applications and reduced emissions, making them suitable for industrial and protective uses in various environments.</p></p>
