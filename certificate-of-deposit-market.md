<p><p>The certificate of deposit (CD) market is categorized into three types based on maturity duration. Short-term CDs typically last from a few months to one year, offering lower interest rates but quick liquidity. Mid-term CDs range from one to five years, providing a balance between interest earnings and access to funds. Long-term CDs extend beyond five years, often featuring higher interest rates, but they tie up capital for an extended period, making them suitable for specific long-term savings goals.</p></p>
