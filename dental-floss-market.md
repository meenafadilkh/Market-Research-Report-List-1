<p><p>The dental floss market is categorized mainly into waxed and unwaxed floss. Waxed floss is coated with a thin layer of wax, making it easier to glide between teeth and suitable for tighter spaces. It is generally more durable and less prone to shredding. Unwaxed floss, on the other hand, is thinner and can be more effective in removing plaque but may shred or break in tight gaps. Both types cater to different consumer preferences and needs for oral hygiene.</p></p>
