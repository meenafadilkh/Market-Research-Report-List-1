<p><p>The differentiated thyroid cancer treatment market encompasses various approaches. Radioiodine ablation targets remaining cancerous cells post-surgery using radioactive iodine. Thyroid stimulating hormone (TSH) suppression involves lowering TSH levels to inhibit cancer growth. Chemotherapy employs cytotoxic drugs to kill cancer cells, typically for advanced cases. Targeted multikinase therapy uses drugs that specifically target molecular pathways in cancer cells, improving efficacy. Other treatments may include external beam radiation or immunotherapy, broadening options for patient management in differentiated thyroid cancer.</p></p>
