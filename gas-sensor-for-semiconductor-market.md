<p><p>The gas sensor market for semiconductors is divided into two main types: resistive and non-resistive sensors. Resistive gas sensors operate by changing their electrical resistance in response to gas exposure, typically using metal oxides, making them suitable for detecting various gases at low concentrations. In contrast, non-resistive sensors, such as optical and electrochemical sensors, rely on different principles, such as light absorption or chemical reactions, to detect gases, offering higher selectivity and sensitivity for specific applications.</p></p>
